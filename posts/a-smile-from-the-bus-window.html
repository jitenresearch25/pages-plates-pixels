A Smile from the Bus Window

A tender memory of teenage love, silence, and growing up.

When I was in school, my family moved to another town. Every day, I travelled by bus. In that bus, I noticed a girl from the same school. I saw her in the morning and again while returning home, and slowly that small journey became the best part of my day.

One day, she noticed me and smiled. For a teenage heart, that smile felt bigger than the whole world. After that, we started talking. Sometimes we sat together. Sometimes we only exchanged a few words. But even those few words were enough to make me happy for the whole day.

When she was absent, the bus felt empty. When she returned, I would ask why she had not come. It was innocent, silent, one-sided love — the kind that lives more in imagination than in reality.

Then one day I saw her with someone else. They were close, talking freely, and I understood something without anyone explaining it to me. Later, I heard her speaking to a friend, and instead of feeling angry, I felt sorry for her. Maybe everyone was confused in their own way. Maybe we were all too young to understand what we were carrying.

So I moved away quietly. I did not fight. I did not complain. Some feelings are not meant to become stories with happy endings. Some stay as soft memories — a bus window, a school road, and one smile that once made an ordinary morning unforgettable.
